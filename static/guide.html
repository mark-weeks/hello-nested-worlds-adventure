How to Play

Enfolded: a living multiverse of eleven nested scales

You are an explorer in a world that was here before you arrived and keeps moving after you leave. Everything you do — every word, every act, every solved puzzle — becomes part of the world's permanent chronicle, and every player and agent who comes after you builds on it.

The world

Reality is nested eleven deep: Multiverse → Universe → Galaxy → Planetary System → Planet → Region → Room → Object → Molecule → Atom → Subatomic Particle. Every place is unique — its name, its properties, its one-line aspect, and its generative art belong to it alone. Descend into children, climb back up, and watch the scenery change register as you cross scales.

Consequences travel

Strong events — a solved puzzle, a scale verb — don't stay where they happen. The origin changes instantly; then the consequence ripples outward at world speed, one ring of scales every few seconds. Solve a puzzle in a Room and watch the Region settle moments later, then the Planet, then the Galaxy. The feed shows each arrival.

The art is the place

Every node draws its own generative art — a pure function of what it is and what has happened to it. Causal pressure saturates the palette and shakes the geometry; a stabilized place wears a halo; corruption glitches the composition; every recorded interaction etches a trace mark. If a place looks weathered, it has been lived in.

You are not alone

Other explorers share the world live — and so does a recurring cast of twelve wandering agents (Tessera, Aunt Entropy, The Locksmith, and the rest), each with their own temperament: tenders, destabilizers, scholars, wanderers. Each keeps to their favorite scales, has their own nerve for danger, and their own verbal habits. They walk the world between your visits, attempt puzzles under the same rules you play by, meet each other, and talk. Their conversations persist — ask a place what it overheard.

The Travelers panel shows everyone abroad right now — humans and the cast alike — and where they stand. Click a traveler to go to them (if they're deeper than your view reaches, the world unfolds to take you there). Solving is better together: everyone at a puzzle shares one pool of attempts, and one correct answer counts for all.

The scales think differently

The eleven scales are not the same game at different zoom levels. Each universe has its own physics — its declared law is real: in one, consequences barely travel; in another they tunnel through scales without touching them; in an Inverted universe, what should rise sinks. You learn a universe's temperament by acting in it and watching what your act does.

The great scales are slow. Kindle a galaxy or calibrate a universe and the act is recorded at once — but the change itself travels on a cosmic clock, settling minutes or hours later, often after you've gone. Plant, leave, return.

The smallest scale gives up on locality. Some particle pairs are entangled: solve one twin's puzzle and the other resolves in the same instant, however far apart they sit. And the cosmic scales pose questions about the fold itself — how many scales hold a place, how many lie within it. A name, read closely, is a map of its whole lineage.

Sealed rooms

Some rooms are sealed. A sealed room won't let you (or anyone) in until its lock is answered — and the key is never written on the door: it's a truth about the place that holds the room, readable one scale up. Speak the key from the threshold and the way opens — for everyone, permanently… until the world's slow entropy re-arms the room and the door seals itself again.

Nothing is lost

The world database is a continuous chronicle across every cohort of players. When you return, your positions, your conversations, and the marks you cut into the walls are still there. Open the Chronicle to read the eras before you — and to see the one being written around you right now.
