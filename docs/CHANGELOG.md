## [Unreleased]

### Added
- **Scale-native verbs — one act per level, the restorative counterpart to decay** (`multiverse/verbs.py`, `POST /act`, all three clients). Each of the 11 scales gains exactly one verb that only works there: *attune* repairs a fraying multiverse, *calibrate* nudges the constants, *kindle* ignites stars, *align* flattens the ecliptic, *seed* wakes life on a barren planet, *ward* lowers a region's danger, *inscribe* cuts a permanent mark into a room, *mend* works an object's damage backward, *catalyze* adds a bond, *excite* blueshifts an atom, *observe* collapses a superposed spin (deterministically per observer+particle). Every verb pushes back against the world's entropy — the decay events corrode, the verbs repair — and each act rides the standard causal rails: a SCALE_ACT chronicle entry, ripple pressure, and a staged cascade that arrives ring by ring. Exposed as a rate-limited `POST /act`, an Act tab in `/app`, an Act mode in the explorer, and an `act` CLI command (typing the verb itself works too); `/world` now carries each node's verb affordance.
- **The world chronicle — continuity made perceivable** (`GET /chronicle`, `multiverse/chronicle.py`, panels in both clients). The permanent record was invisible beyond a short feed backfill; now the full history pages backward in time (cursor on mutation id) with the world's total event count and first-event date, and every entry is stamped with a deterministic era name derived from (seed, ISO week) — "The Vigil of Emberglass" reads identically to every participant forever. Both clients gain a Chronicle panel with era headers and "further back" pagination.
- **Agent-to-agent life** (`agents/banter.py`, `server/heartbeat.py`). Co-located wanderers now actually converse: a deterministic in-character exchange (persona-voiced opener, response, closing stage direction) grounded in the node's real state — danger, condition, stability, wards, inscriptions, causal pressure — at zero API cost. Roughly a third of heartbeat ticks are social (a second roster wanderer loiters at the walker's drop-in node so meetings genuinely happen). Every meeting persists as an AGENT_TALK chronicle entry, broadcasts live as chat-styled lines, and enters the node's memory — places now allude to conversations they overheard when spoken to.
- **Browser E2E smoke in CI** (`frontend/e2e/`, `playwright.config.js`, third CI job). Headless Chromium loads both clients from the real Python server under the production CSP, walking the real first-run onboarding: the explorer's sigil must paint opaque pixels, the Act panel must carry the scale verb, the chronicle must open, and the Pixi canvas must mount under `script-src 'self'`; any pageerror or console.error fails the run. This is the layer that would have caught the CSP-blocked-PixiJS blank scene.
- **Ambient sound — the audible face of the node art** (`static/nodesound.js`, toggles in both clients). Pure WebAudio synthesis, deterministic in (seed, node): register follows scale depth (a Multiverse drones at 55 Hz, a particle sings at 880), the node's hue swing sets the beat between its two voices, causal jitter becomes tremolo, danger roughens the tone unless the place was stabilized. No assets, no network, off by default (the toggle is the browser-required activation gesture).
- **Player guide** (`static/guide.html`, served ungated at `/guide`). A how-to-play page — the eleven scales, the verbs table, how consequences travel, what the art means, the wandering cast, the continuity promise — linked from both intros and the `/app` status bar.
- **Client error reporting** (`POST /client-error`, both clients). Browser crashes were invisible (Sentry is server-side only); `window.onerror` and unhandled rejections now POST to a rate-limited, size-capped, log-only sink, so a broken deploy shows up in `fly logs`.

### Changed
- **History pruning is continuity-gated** (`persistence`). `NESTED_WORLDS_MUTATION_TTL_DAYS` alone no longer prunes `world_mutations` — the chronicle is policy-protected — unless `NESTED_WORLDS_ALLOW_HISTORY_PRUNE=1` explicitly confirms; a bare TTL logs a warning and does nothing.
- **Deploys are scripted with the backup first** (`scripts/deploy.sh`). The continuity policy's backup-before-every-deploy rule is now the easy path: online backup (refusing to deploy on failure), prune of old machine-local backups, then `fly deploy`; `--first-deploy` covers the bootstrap case.
- **WebSocket reconnects back off exponentially** (both clients). The React client's fixed 3s retry and the explorer's *no retry at all* are replaced with a jittered exponential ladder (1s → 30s cap, reset on success).
- **Reduced motion is honored** (both clients): causal flashes and transient overlays become no-ops under `prefers-reduced-motion`; the sigil and scene canvases carry `role="img"` labels built from the node's aspect.
- **Every node is unique** (`multiverse/generator.py`). Node identity is now synthesized, not drawn from small pools: name bases are built per level from syllable roots/mids/endings, fused word-pairs, and place nouns (~10⁵ combinations per level, always ≥1 mid-syllable so the space never collapses), every node carries a synthesized `aspect` — a one-line sensory description composed from detail/texture/motion/mood banks — and each of the 11 levels rolls an extra categorical property plus a continuous one (membrane textures and hum periods for multiverses, skies and day lengths for planets, air and ceiling heights for rooms, glows and resonance wavelengths for atoms…). Across a full 3000-node world, complete property fingerprints never repeat and ~98% of name bases are distinct (guarded by `tests/test_generator.py::TestNodeUniqueness`); the path suffix stays on the full name, so names remain canonical, stable, and resolvable in O(depth).
- **Consequences travel at world speed — staged causal cascades** (`causality/staging.py`, `persistence/migrations/0007_causal_queue.sql`, `server/heartbeat.py`). A solve no longer hits all eleven scales in the same instant: only the origin's immediate ring fires synchronously in the triggering request; every farther ring is written to the durable `causal_queue` with a due time and drained by a pump thread (one ring per hop delay, default 12s, `NESTED_WORLDS_HOP_DELAY`; `NESTED_WORLDS_CAUSAL_PUMP=0` disables). Each arriving hop runs through the standard causal wiring (history + ripple + property effects), is broadcast live to the seed-room as it lands, and chains the next ring — so connected players watch a consequence ripple outward over a minute rather than blink everywhere at once. Staged hops persist: a restart delays a ripple, never loses it. An equivalence test proves a fully drained staged cascade reaches exactly the nodes at exactly the strengths the old synchronous propagation did (`tests/test_causal_delay.py`).
- **Per-node generative art** (`static/nodeart.js`, `static/nodeart-global.js`, `frontend/src/components/SceneView.jsx`, `static/explorer.js`). Every node now has unique visual identity that is a pure function of (seed, name, properties, history) — one deterministic ES module shared verbatim by both clients (imported by React, exposed as `window.NodeArt` for the D3 explorer). Eleven per-scale form families (membrane folds, filaments, spirals, orbital rings, planet horizons, region ridges, room panels, object sigils, molecular bonds, electron shells, quantum speckle) composed by a mulberry32 PRNG seeded from an FNV-1a hash of the node's identity; the node's history is legible in the art — causal pressure saturates the palette and jitters the geometry, `stabilized` draws a halo, `corrupted` glitches the composition with displaced slices, high `danger_level` vignettes the edges, and accumulated interactions etch activity marks (the `/world` payload now carries per-node `activity` counts from `world_mutations`). In `/app` the art is the always-present scene base layer at zero API spend, with fal.ai imagery blended over it as a translucent wash when available; the explorer renders it as a node sigil panel. No `Math.random`/`Date.now` anywhere in the module — determinism is the contract (17 Vitest tests: reproducibility, distinctness across nodes/seeds, all 11 families distinct, property expression, render smoke tests).
- **Continuity policy — the world is a chronicle, not a per-cohort scratchpad** (`docs/roadmap/phase-2-scale.md`, `README.md`). Cross-cohort persistence is now an explicit product goal: each new player, human or agent, builds on the experience created by everyone before them. The old "each beta cohort gets a fresh DB" non-goal is replaced by a standing policy — never wipe the DB between cohorts, migrations are additive-only, and `python main.py backup` runs before every deploy.

- **Agents are addressable** (`server/handlers.py`, `consciousness.voice_agent(agent_memory=…)`, both clients). The speak panel now lists the presences whose traces sit in the current node's history ("Tessera · scholar") and lets the player address them; `/agent/voice` grounds the reply in that agent's own persisted memory — how many places it knows in this world, whether it has stood HERE before, its recent movements — so "the Tessera whose traces are in this room" answers as a returning presence, not an improvised stranger. New `GET /history?node_name=` returns node-scoped history for the presence list. Fallback stays in fiction ("Tessera does not answer. Only the traces of a scholar remain…").
- **Passage affordances** (`frontend/src/badges.js`, `TextPanel`, `SceneView` hotspot studs, explorer affordance rings, CLI `look` tags). Passages now signal what is mechanically notable through them — high danger (with the level), corruption, unrest, stabilization, accumulated causal pressure, locks — as small badges/marks in every client, turning undifferentiated clicking into discovery. Ubiquitous traits are deliberately un-badged (every node has a puzzle; an affordance that is always on says nothing). Cross-client parity is executed, not grepped: the JS tests run the explorer's hand-mirrored `nodeMark()` against the React `passageBadges()` on the same inputs.
- **The prompt cache finally fires — by making the voices deeper** (`consciousness.LEVEL_LORE`, `_WORLD_CRAFT`, `_AGENT_CRAFT`, `_ARCHETYPE_EXEMPLARS`, shared style rules). Both cached bibles were enriched past the real 4096-token Opus minimum with content that earns its tokens: per-scale lore (working diction, how each scale senses its container and contents, how causal pressure feels, an exemplar exchange), craft sections keyed to the real dynamic-context fields (memory lines, effect properties like `stabilized`/`disturbed`/`corrupted`, causal pressure), puzzle etiquette (never reveal answers, in character), the named wanderer cast as recognized regulars, and archetype exemplars. After the first call in a 1h window, the prefix bills at the ~10x cache-read discount; `tests/test_consciousness.py::TestBibleCacheEffectiveness` guards against regressing below the minimum. The canonical wanderer cast now lives in `consciousness.WANDERER_CAST` (the heartbeat imports it), so prompts and ambient agents cannot drift apart.

### Changed
- **Conversation transcripts key on the invite credential** (`persistence.get_player_exchanges(identity)`, `/speak`). The per-(node, speaker) transcript identity is a hash of the per-user invite key when one is presented (display-name fallback for keyless/local play), so two players who both call themselves "Ada" no longer share a memory, and renaming yourself no longer orphans yours. The identity travels in the `PLAYER_SPEAK` payload; `json_extract` is noted in the SQL dialect seam.
- Clients send only `(seed, node_name)` to `/speak` and `/image` — the previously ignored `node_id`/`node_level`/`node_properties` body fields are gone from both request paths.

### Fixed
- **The `/app` scene renderer was dead under production CSP — every deployed scene was blank** (`frontend/src/main.jsx`). PixiJS v8 compiles shader/uniform glue with `new Function`, which the server's Content-Security-Policy (`script-src 'self'`, no `unsafe-eval`) rightly blocks — so `Application.init()` rejected on every production load, the silent catch ate it, and the immersive client rendered no canvas at all (it only ever worked on the CSP-less Vite dev server, which is why it kept passing local checks). Importing Pixi's `pixi.js/unsafe-eval` compatibility module replaces the eval paths; the CSP stays strict. Found because the init catch now logs a warning instead of swallowing the reason (`SceneView.jsx`).
- **Destroying a mid-init Pixi app crashed SceneView** (`frontend/src/components/SceneView.jsx`). `app.destroy()` in the effect cleanup ran while `Application.init()` was still in flight, throwing `this._cancelResize is not a function` from Pixi's resize plugin — an uncaught error with no boundary above it (React StrictMode's dev double-mount hit it every time; any fast unmount could in production). Cleanup now defers the destroy until init settles.
- **The explorer's first-load sigil was blank** (`static/explorer.js`, `static/nodeart-global.js`). The art ships as a deferred ES module, so the initial entry selection could run before `window.NodeArt` existed and nothing ever redrew. The shim now announces itself (`nodeart-ready`) and the explorer redraws the selected node's sigil the moment the art is available.
- **The WebSocket layer now speaks real RFC 6455, and one stalled client can't freeze a room** (`server/protocol.py`, `server/rooms.py`, `server/handlers.py`). Framing: unmasked client frames are rejected as protocol errors with a 1002 close (§5.1) instead of quietly accepted; fragmented messages are reassembled into one payload (§5.4) instead of each fragment being mangled into a separate "message"; pings are answered with matching pongs (§5.5.2) — including pings interleaved mid-fragmentation — and close frames are echoed so the closing handshake completes. Broadcast: each connected player now has a dedicated writer thread draining a bounded outbox, so `broadcast()` only enqueues — previously it did a blocking `sendall` per player in sequence, letting one client with a full TCP window stall every message to everyone in the room. A player whose outbox overflows is marked dead and evicted; a per-player send lock keeps reader-thread control echoes from interleaving bytes with writer-thread data frames.
- **Solved puzzles survive restarts** (`server/rooms.py::_new_session`, `persistence.get_puzzle_solve`). Co-op `PuzzleSession` state is per-process memory, but a solve is a durable fact: a fresh room now rehydrates solver + contributors from the persisted `PUZZLE_SOLVED` mutation, so a deploy no longer resets solved puzzles against a history that says they're solved. Ambient agents' solves deliberately do NOT claim the co-op session — wanderers must not lock puzzles away from players.

### Added (previous release)
- **The world runs unattended — ambient heartbeat** (`server/heartbeat.py`, `server/__init__.py`). A daemon loop (default every 180s; `NESTED_WORLDS_HEARTBEAT=0` disables, `NESTED_WORLDS_HEARTBEAT_INTERVAL` tunes) sends a recurring cast of persona agents (*Tessera, Halden, Mirrorbird, …*) on paced traversals of inhabited or recently visited worlds. Each tick persists history/ripple/effects through the standard wiring and broadcasts `causal_event`/`agent_encounter`/`agent_done` live to the seed-room, so connected players watch the world move and returning players find it changed. Heartbeat agents are FSM-driven — zero Anthropic/fal.ai spend. Covered by `tests/test_heartbeat.py` (persistent traces, live broadcast frames, room-registry hygiene, memory accretion across ticks).
- **Causal events change world substance** (`multiverse/effects.py`, `causality/wiring.py`, migration `0006_property_overrides.sql`). Events at or above 0.3 strength now have material consequences: `PUZZLE_SOLVED` sets `stabilized` and calms `danger_level` (a solved room puzzle settles its region via the first upward hop); `DANGER_ALERT` raises danger or marks `disturbed`; `STRUCTURAL_CHANGE` degrades `condition` (pristine → worn → damaged → corrupted). Changes persist as a JSON property overlay on `node_runtime_state`, applied on top of deterministic generation at every rebuild — the world every participant sees carries what has happened in it, and `/world` payloads now expose per-node `ripple_score`. `causality/wiring.py::wire_world_handlers` is the single standard wiring (record + additive ripple + effects) used by the server, the heartbeat, and the CLI alike.
- **Node memory with content, and real conversations** (`consciousness/__init__.py`, `persistence.get_player_exchanges`). The `PLAYER_SPEAK` mutation now stores both sides of the exchange; `_history_block` renders what visitors said and what the node answered (instead of bare event stubs); a per-(node, player) transcript is passed to `speak()` as genuine multi-turn messages, so the second conversation knows the first happened; and persisted causal pressure reaches the voice (`_ripple_line`). History window raised to 10 with a deterministic id tiebreak.
- **The failure voice** (`consciousness.LEVEL_FALLBACKS`, `fallback_voice()`). When the Claude API is unavailable (no key, SDK/network failure), every scale answers with an authored line of silence in its own register — HTTP 200 with `ai: false`, never a 503, never an SDK exception at a player. The CLI does the same, and the prompt-cache operator warning now fires only at server startup (`server.run`), never into a player's terminal.
- **JS test infrastructure** (`frontend/src/__tests__/`, Vitest, CI `npm test`). Behavior tests for entry resolution (including a cross-client parity test that EXECUTES the hand-mirrored `static/explorer.js` copies against `frontend/src/entry.js` on the same inputs) and for WebSocket dispatch — the layer whose silent gap shipped the dropped-welcome bug.

### Changed
- **One canonical world per seed** (`multiverse/generator.py`). Every node is now a pure function of (seed, path): name, properties, and branching come from a per-node SHA-256-seeded RNG, so **any depth prefix is identical to the full-depth world** — `/agent` (depth 11), the browser clients (depth 6), and the CLI finally traverse the same world per seed, and persistence keyed on (seed, node_name) refers to the same place everywhere. Names encode their path (`Vault-1231` ⇒ 1→2→3→1), which makes them unique, stable across rebuilds, and resolvable in O(depth) without generating the tree (`resolve_node_by_name`). Molecule/Atom/SubatomicParticle gained name pools; atoms are physically coherent (element matches atomic number); planet population follows habitation. Breaking: node names/layouts differ from previous seeds' trees.
- **Node identity is server-derived** (`server/handlers.py`). `/speak`, `/image`, and `/agent/voice` resolve the client-named node against the canonical world (404 for forged names) and derive level/properties/ripple/history server-side — a request can no longer forge a node's nature, style-inject `/image`, or write history for places that don't exist. `/agent/voice` now stands on a real node with its real history. `seed` defaults to 42 (the shared default world), eliminating the silent amnesiac `seed=0` path.
- **Broadcast strengths are the engine's truth** (`server/handlers.py`, `interface/__init__.py`). SSE/WS causal events and the CLI ambient bars now carry `event.strength` — the actual propagated, dampened value — instead of a display-side `0.6^depth` recomputation, and hop distance uses a full bidirectional distance map (`build_distance_map`), so upward-cascading events no longer display at strength 1.0.
- **Agents play by the rules** (`agents/agent.py`). On a `has_puzzle` node an agent attempts the node's actual engine puzzle with difficulty-weighted odds ({1: 0.75 … 4: 0.2}, reproducible per (agent, node)) and can fail — `PUZZLE_FAILED` included; the free-`PUZZLE_SOLVED`-for-standing-there rule asymmetry is gone. Danger alerts now `propagate` upward instead of firing in place. Agent memory is keyed by node NAME (stable across rebuilds) and the traversal budget counts FRESH visits, fixing the bug where restored memory consumed the whole budget and every named agent went permanently inert on its second run.
- **Both clients show a world already in motion** (`frontend/src/dispatch.js`, `App.jsx`, `static/explorer.js`). The React client now consumes the `welcome` roster (players present before you connected were previously invisible until they acted) and upserts unknown movers; both clients backfill the world's recent past from `/history` into the event feed on load ("Tessera passed through Vault-12", "danger stirred at The Mire-111"); both render accumulated causal pressure on the node panel; `/app` gained the first-run intro; the explorer's puzzle panel now trusts the server's pooled co-op attempt counts instead of a local counter that drifted whenever a partner guessed.
- **CLI is part of the shared world** (`interface/__init__.py`, `main.py`). Play-mode puzzle solves persist and cascade (with the standard wiring); ambient observation leaves real traces; the session tree hydrates persisted evolution; `play --name` gives you an identity nodes remember; `--seed` is accepted after subcommands; `puzzles` plays a bounded tour (`--limit`, default 10) with skip/quit and EOF safety instead of an uninterruptible 1,700-puzzle march that crashed on Ctrl-D; `history` surfaces puzzle results (the previously write-only table).

### Fixed
- **Puzzle answers no longer leak through node properties** (`puzzles/engine.py`). Attached `Puzzle` objects (whose repr includes the answer and every hint) lived in `node.properties`, where the CLI `look` printed them and `speak()` serialized them into the node's own system prompt — one `puzzle` command and the node could be asked for its answer. Puzzles now live in the engine, keyed by node name; guarded by regression tests.
- **`worlds.created_at` survives revisits** (`persistence.save_world`): `INSERT OR REPLACE` deleted + reinserted the row on every `/world` call, re-firing the timestamp default — the world's birth date was unrecoverable. Now an upsert that preserves it.
- **Ripple lost-update race** (`persistence.increment_ripple_score`): concurrent participants' causal pressure now compounds via an atomic DB-side increment (clamped at 1.0) instead of last-writer-wins absolute upserts from each request's private tree.
- **WebSocket handshake speaks HTTP/1.1** (`server/handlers.py`): the 101 upgrade previously arrived as `HTTP/1.0`, which spec-strict clients (e.g. Python `websockets`) reject. Ordinary responses carry `Connection: close`, preserving one-request-per-connection semantics.
- Dead weight removed: `Persona.voice_preamble` (documented as "injected into consciousness system prompt", never consumed — the live archetype text is `consciousness._AGENT_ARCHETYPES`), `SpatialNode.interaction_summary` (written nowhere), the unused `BIOME_CLUES`/`GALAXY_SHAPE_CLUES`/`PARTICLE_TYPE_CLUES` maps, and the `_MIN_STRENGTH` alias. Easter-egg pointers repaired (`first-lock.md` command path; `lockbox.js` now points at the served `/easter-egg/illusion`). Stale docs reconciled (ripple persistence note in `game-design.md`, co-op status in `phase-1-beta.md`, invite-URL note in this changelog's earlier entry).

### Added (previous unreleased)
- **Server-side cross-device resume, keyed on the invite key** (`persistence/migrations/0005_player_position.sql` (new), `persistence/__init__.py`, `server/handlers.py`, `frontend/src/App.jsx`, `static/explorer.js`). Resume now follows a tester across devices and browsers, not just across sessions in the same browser. The per-user invite key is already a stable identity, so the last position hangs off it: migration 0005 adds `last_node` + the four world columns (`last_seed`/`last_depth`/`last_min_breadth`/`last_max_breadth`) and a `last_node_at` timestamp to the `invite_keys` table, and `persistence.save_player_position` / `get_player_position` write and read them. A new `GET /position` returns the caller's saved position and `POST /position` records it, both keyed on `guard.supplied_key(...)`. Because the columns live on `invite_keys`, server-side resume is *naturally scoped to per-user keys*: a shared-env-key or no-key session has no row, so the write no-ops (`{"saved": false}`) and the read returns `null` — the client silently falls back to its own `localStorage` cache, exactly as before. Both clients now hydrate from the server on boot (`GET /position` before the local resume path runs) and mirror every move back (`POST /position`, fire-and-forget), so `localStorage` stays a same-browser cache while the server is the cross-device source of truth. Verified end-to-end in headless Chromium: tester "Ada" moved to a chosen node on one browser, and a brand-new browser context carrying the same key — with empty `localStorage` and a *different* player name — resumed on that exact node rather than dropping in fresh; the React `/app` client then hydrated to the same node the D3 explorer had saved (cross-client too). New tests cover the persistence layer (`tests/test_persistence.py::TestPlayerPosition` — round-trip, per-key isolation, and no-op for empty/unknown/revoked keys), the endpoint (`tests/test_server.py::TestPositionResume` — round-trip keyed by invite key, isolation between keys, and no-op for no-key / shared-env-key sessions), and the client wiring in both frontends plus the built bundle (`tests/test_frontend_contract.py::TestCrossDeviceResume`).
- **Non-linear entry: drop-in on first visit, resume across sessions** (`frontend/src/entry.js` (new), `frontend/src/App.jsx`, `static/explorer.js`). Traversal is non-linear — every client moves up or down from any node and there is no "reach the bottom" goal — so the clients no longer pin entry to the root. A **first-time player is dropped in at a node in the middle of the world** (one with both a parent and children, so exploration in either direction is immediate), chosen deterministically from their name via a shared FNV-1a hash, so a given player has a stable arrival point and the two clients agree on it. A **returning player resumes exactly where they left off**: the current node is persisted to `localStorage` on every move, along with the world it belonged to (seed/depth/breadth in the D3 explorer, seed in `/app`), and on load the client restores that world and reopens at the saved node — falling back to a fresh drop-in if the node is no longer present. The initial drop-in / resume position is broadcast over the WebSocket on connect (`ws.onopen` in the explorer; a `connected`-keyed effect in `/app`) so other players see the arriving player where they actually are; the D3 explorer also pans the view to centre the entry node. Pure entry-resolution logic (`findPath` / `dropInNode` / `entryPath`) lives in `frontend/src/entry.js` and is mirrored in `static/explorer.js`. Verified end-to-end in headless Chromium for both clients: a first-time player drops into a non-root node (Planet · Droven-13 for the sample world), the same player deterministically drops into the same node on a cleared browser, and a reload after moving resumes the moved-to node. New `tests/test_frontend_contract.py::TestNonLinearEntry` guards the wiring in both source clients and the built bundle.

### Changed
- **Rebranded to _Enfolded: Nested World Adventure_** (`README.md`, `pyproject.toml`, `server/__init__.py`, `interface/__init__.py`, `main.py`, `frontend/`, `static/`, `setup.sh`, `Dockerfile`, `fly.toml`, docs). The game is renamed from *Nested Worlds Adventure* to **Enfolded: Nested World Adventure**; the title references David Bohm's implicate order (*Wholeness and the Implicate Order*, 1980) — every part enfolds the whole. Primary domain is now [enfolded.world](https://enfolded.world). All user-facing titles, banners, and the join/name modals in both browser clients now read "Enfolded" (the `/app` bundle was rebuilt), and the README carries a Bohm epigraph. The `NESTED_WORLDS_*` environment-variable prefix is deliberately **unchanged** to avoid breaking deployed instances.
- **Puzzles redesigned: non-trivial, non-leaking, node-unique, difficulty-curved** (`puzzles/generators.py` (new), `puzzles/engine.py`, `tests/test_puzzles.py`). The pre-beta review found the property-keyed dynamic generators were both trivial and repetitive: answers were stated in the prompt and spelled out in the hints, ~22% of answers (139/629 in a sample tree) were a value the node already ships in its `/world` payload, and puzzles collapsed across nodes — `The Widdershins Door` (`min(3, exits)`, three possible answers ever) appeared ~150 times in one tree, and 35% of all puzzles were exact duplicates of another node. Replaced with a per-node generator: scale-themed anagrams and Caesar ciphers (answer hidden behind a transform the player reverses), inferred numeric sequences (answer hidden behind a rule), and the existing hand-written riddles/logic reused after filtering out any that leak their answer. Selection is seeded from the node's own identity, so each node's puzzle is unique to it yet reproducible — co-op players on a node see the same puzzle, and a rebuilt world regenerates it identically (`node_rng`). **Difficulty is a per-node property, not a depth curve.** Traversal is non-linear — players drop in at any node and move up or down, exploring continuously with no "reach the bottom" goal — so a depth curve would wall anyone who drops into a deep scale, make challenge yo-yo as they wander, and imply a false end-goal. Instead each node draws its own difficulty (1 gentle … 4 hard) seeded from its identity and spread across the whole range at *every* scale (`node_difficulty`); scale sets a puzzle's flavour, never how hard it is. Fairness travels with each puzzle — graduated hints, and more attempts for harder ones. The difficulty is surfaced on the `/puzzle` response and rendered as a ★ rating in both frontends so players can pick their own challenge while exploring. A selector-level guard rejects any candidate whose answer would surface in the prompt (e.g. a numeric answer colliding with the node-name index) and falls back to a guaranteed-clean anagram. Measured after the change (same sample tree, three seeds): 0 answers in prompt / hints / properties, 0 unsolvable, ~82–85% distinct puzzles with no single puzzle exceeding a small fraction of the tree, 110–124 distinct challenge types per world, and the full 1–4 difficulty spread present at both the top (Multiverse) and bottom (SubatomicParticle) scales. Server-validated attempts still never send the answer to the client. `tests/test_puzzles.py` was rewritten from per-generator formula assertions into quality invariants (no-leak, solvable-by-own-answer, graduated hints, per-node determinism, low cross-node repetition, per-node difficulty spread, and anagram/cipher transform integrity); the co-op server test now derives the expected answer through the deterministic generator instead of the old `danger_level × 2` formula.
